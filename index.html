02 The Subject

Why this calculator exists.

The default iOS calculator works. It also looks like nothing, feels like nothing, and asks nothing of you. Calco was made for the moments when that is not enough. When you want the satisfaction of pressing a key that presses back. When you want a display that glows the way an old desk calculator glowed. When you want a tool that respects the small act of doing math.

Seven modes. One unhurried surface. Basic and scientific. Currency and units. A finance solver for the spreadsheet hours, a date calculator for the planning hours, a percent calculator for the dinners. All under one panel of brushed silver aluminium that gets out of the way and lets you feel the keys.

Priced once. Sold once. Nothing renews. The economics match the object: pay for what was made, then keep it for as long as you want it.

03 The Material
  • i.

    Brushed silver aluminium panel

    A single milled surface holds the entire keypad. The grain runs left to right; light catches it the way it catches a watch case.

  • ii.

    Sculpted rounded-square keys

    Each key is a soft-cornered rectangle, set just proud of the panel. They press back when you tap, with a custom haptic tuned for the hand.

  • iii.

    Dark-teal LCD with green emissive digits

    The display is calibrated to look like the LCD from a 1980s Casio MS-80, with a slight glow on each digit. Sharp at any distance, gentle in any light.

  • iv.

    Warm-orange operator column

    The same warm orange that has lived on Casio keypads since 1985, applied with intent. The equals key rewards a press with both colour and haptic.

04 In Use

For the hours that ask for math.

The kind of math that gets done in studios, kitchens, on trains, at the corner of a table. Currency in a borrowed café, a tip in a restaurant that runs cash only, a TVM solve in a quiet morning, a date interval right before a deadline. The calculator that comes with the phone does not honour those moments. This one tries to.

05 Two States

Warm by day.
Neon by night.

Two finished themes, both shipped, both considered. The Warm theme borrows from a desk lamp at 4pm. The Neon theme borrows from a single brass lamp at midnight. You can pick one, or let iOS choose for you.

06 Heritage

The line, from 1985 to today.

The Casio MS-80 came out of an industrial design tradition that wanted tools to feel honest. Cream plastic, grey rubber, a small pink C key, an LCD that glowed exactly enough. It sat next to notepads in offices for forty years. Calco is what that object would look like if it were rebuilt today for iOS.

The warmth of a 1980s desk calculator. The precision of a modern OS.
